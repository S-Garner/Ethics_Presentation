The Ethics of Social Media Algorithms

Seth Garner

Importance

  • Pervasive in all facets
  • 90% use it for news and happenings Pew Research, 2021
  • Digital platforms dominate attention, shape perspectives, and filter visibility

Curated Culture

  • These tools have allowed for greater reach of perspective and voices
  • Shaping of culture
  • We're shown what's popular to others, not what's relevant to you
  • A growing and shrinking Internet

Algorithmic Measures

  • Less platform diversity, more consolidated control
  • Retention is key, TikTok has been shown to completely disregard likes and follows, they base what they recommend almost purely on 'seconds watched'(The Wall Street Journal, 2021)
  • Inaction is still action
  • Filter bubbles reinforce echo chambers and limit opposing views
  • Black box and transparency
  • If you're not paying for the product, you are the product

Bots and Exploits

  • Algorithms tend to gradually nudge
  • ~ 15% of X users are bots (University of Southern California, 2017)
  • Anyone can buy bots to simulate natural engagement or spread disinformation

Effects

  • A little bit of everything, all of the time
  • FOMO and Comparison
  • Which could lead to compulsive buying due to constant trend chasing
  • Doomscrolling, Ambient Awareness
  • ‚ÄúPlatforms fuel obsessive brand passion by triggering the fear of missing out.‚Äù ‚Äî Journal of Business Research, 2024

Bias, Belief, and Bubbles

  • Disinformation spreads 6x faster than the truth on Twitter (MIT, 2018)
  • Filter bubbles trap users in ideological loops and warped world views
  • Social influence biases (Posts with more likes are seen as more trustworthy, regardless of accuracy)
  • Algorithmic radicalization on YouTube has been linked to extremist ideologies, especially to young men(NYT, 2019)

Disproportionate Impact on Diverse Users

  • Black women are often flattened into harmful stereotypes ‚Äî either as aggressive (when using AAVE) or hypersexualized (through algorithmic emphasis on body imagery)(Algorithms of Oppression, 2018)
  • "Google creates advertising algorithms, not information algorithms." - Safiya Umoja Noble
  • Digital erasure
  • The intrinsic nature of addictive algorithms on youth and groups that do not realize manipulation

Solutions

  • Ethical audits
  • Better transparency
  • Labeling > Removing: Flag information instead of deleting it
  • Better user agency and control
